## 17:20 — The presence shelf was boosting the exact vocal-presence range it was meant to protect
**Follow-up on the solver-inefficiency lead from the 16:10 entry.** Traced the ~1dB
gap between idealized (2.3-2.8dB) and observed (3.1-4.0dB) required boost to its
mechanism: `_smooth_band_gain`'s logistic ramp for the presence shelf was CENTERED
at 3000Hz (`lo_hz=3000.0`, width 540Hz) -- the same frequency the gate's
`high3000_share` measures against. Correction to the first pass at this root cause:
the gate uses `freqs > 3000`, so the bin at exactly 3000Hz is already excluded from
the measurement and is not itself the defect. The actual inefficiency is the WIDTH of
the transition straddling that boundary: bins just above 3000Hz (counted in the
numerator) were receiving only partial gain since full plateau wasn't reached until
~4000-4600Hz, while bins below 3000Hz (never counted in the numerator, since they're
excluded by `freqs > 3000`) were still receiving real collateral gain from the same
logistic tail -- inflating the denominator (total power) without contributing
anything to the numerator. Both effects pull the ratio the wrong way at once.

**Asked planner:** the exact filter code, the exact gate measurement code, the
constraint (a "presence/air" shelf must not brighten the 1-3kHz vocal-intelligibility
range), and the naive fix's obvious problem (shifting the ramp low enough to be ~95%
by 3000Hz would center it around 1400-1800Hz, deep in protected territory).

**Planner said:** worse than inefficiency -- computed the logistic's actual 10-90%
transition span (with the real w=540Hz) as ~1.81-4.19kHz, meaning the shelf was
ALREADY boosting real energy in the 1.4-3kHz range it was supposed to protect. Fix:
a lower-knee-anchored shelf (zero gain AT/below 3000Hz, full plateau by ~4000Hz,
log-frequency + dB-domain interpolated smootherstep, not a centered logistic), PLUS
replace the idealized closed-form `_solve()` iteration with a response-aware solver
(brentq root-find against the real per-bin power and the real shelf response, not an
assumed step function). Explicitly advised against moving to a biquad filter (same
fundamental solver-vs-processor mismatch, plus adds phase rotation) and against
accepting the gap as "the honest cost of smoothness" (real waste is the collateral
lift, not smoothness itself).

**Did:** `earcrate/judge/audio.py` -- added `_presence_shelf_weight()` (quintic
smootherstep, log-frequency, 3000-4000Hz knees), `_presence_shelf_gain()` (dB-domain
interpolation), `_solve_presence_shelf_db()` (brentq against actual per-bin STFT
power, same 4096/2048 definition `_band_shares` uses, returns `None` — not a clamped
guess — when unreachable within the persona-derived cap). Rewrote `_apply()` to take
`high_shelf_db` instead of a linear `high_amp` ratio. Replaced the 3-iteration
approximate high-band loop with a single correct solve + apply + remeasure-verify.
Removed the now-dead `HIGH_GAIN_LIMIT`.

**Verified:**

- `pytest tests/test_gates.py -q` — 75 passed, same 3 pre-existing unrelated
  failures (git-stash-confirmed baseline, unchanged from earlier entries today).
- Direct frequency-response check: `_presence_shelf_gain([...], shelf_db=3.0)` ->
  exactly +0.000dB at 1000/1600/2500/2999/3000Hz, ramping to +3.000dB by 4000Hz.
  Zero collateral lift into protected territory, confirmed numerically, not assumed.
